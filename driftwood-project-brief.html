02 — Concept
The elevated dive bar.
Curated. Moody. Lived-in. Not heavy. Not grimy. Not trying.

Driftwood reads like a place that already had a life before you walked in. Worn timber, dim corners, things on the walls that look collected rather than bought. The trick is that none of it is accidental — every move is paid for in dwell time, return visits and spend-per-head.

This is not a dive bar. It is the version of a dive bar a designer would draw if you asked them to make somewhere honest. Clientele skews late-twenties to forties, early-evening through late-night, drinks-led with snack-grade food.

The room earns its rent on atmosphere over fit-out spend. Lighting, palette, sound and a few hero objects do the heavy lifting.

04 — Budget Approach
Spend where the punter feels it.
Box Clever.
01

Lighting carries the room

Warm-dim LED zoning, gels on architectural fittings, neon as feature. A good lighting scheme on cheap walls beats a poor scheme on panelled ones. Largest single line on the FF&E — protected.

02

Paint over plaster

Deep desaturated tones forgive uneven walls, hide dated joinery and read intentional. Existing fabric kept where it can be reframed. No structural moves unless they unlock revenue.

03

Hero objects, not hero finishes

One pinball machine and a neon sign do more for spend-per-head than another £8k of joinery. Sourced second-hand, restored where needed, badged as character.

04

FF&E mixed, not matched

Auction houses, salvage yards, local makers. A coherent set of mismatched chairs reads more like a tenant operator than a flat-pack landlord refit. Reduces lead time and cost in one move.

05

Foliage as cladding

Layered greenery softens hard surfaces and breaks sightlines without joinery cost. Specified for a maintenance-light handover — no tenant should inherit a forest they cannot keep.

06

Sound, not silence

A modest specified sound system is FF&E-grade kit. The room should sound like it knows itself before the first pour. Spec to leased-estate compliance, not festival-grade.

05 — Key Elements
Six moves that carry the room.
Anchor · 01

Pinball & arcade

One restored pinball machine in the back room. Optional second-hand cocktail-table arcade as a future tenant add. Generates dwell time and a reason to return without reaching for the pricing list.

Anchor · 02

Hero neon

One commissioned neon piece behind the back bar. Text TBC at tenant signing. Doubles as primary feature lighting — saves a downlight specification.

Anchor · 03

Vintage pendant cluster

Mismatched pendants over the bar pass and hero booth. Coordinated by warmth and brass tones. Sourced via auction over six weeks — tracked separately on the FF&E sheet.

Anchor · 04

Warm-dim LED zoning

Three lighting zones — entrance, bar, back room. Each on its own dimming circuit. The room should be re-readable across trading hours without changing fittings.

Anchor · 05

Botanical layering

Concentrated foliage at sightline-breakers — bar back, booth ends, threshold. Maintenance brief left with the operator at handover. Specified for low-light tolerance.

Anchor · 06

Joinery, restrained

Existing bar retained where structurally sound. Top re-clad, fascia restained, brass detailing added. New joinery confined to back-bar shelving and one banquette run.

06 — Reference
Look at this and nothing else.
The Lioness
of Leith.Edinburgh.
Why
Single most accurate read on the elevated dive-bar mood we are aiming for. Density without weight. Fun without try-hard.
What works
Object density, palette discipline, lighting zoning, music programme, casual food offer, low-effort entry threshold.
What we shift
Glasgow audience, leased-estate constraints, no live tenant. Concept tightened so it survives a tenant we have not yet met.
Avoid
Pastiche, over-styling, anything that reads as themed. Driftwood is not a theme bar.
07 — Terry's Remit
Three lanes. Hold them.
Lane · 01

Intelligence.

Site visits, audience read, competitor scan in the immediate Glasgow catchment. Feed back to design with operator-grade observation — footfall patterns, comparable spend-per-head, dwell windows.

Lane · 02

FF&E sourcing.

Auction routes, salvage, second-hand restoration. Pinball, pendants, seating, neon commission. Track lead times against the programme — protect the long-lead items first.

Lane · 03

Admin.

Asana hygiene, supplier comms, schedule maintenance, handover documentation for the eventual tenant. The brief lives or dies by the paperwork no one wants to do.
