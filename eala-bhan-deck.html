Direction

Vintage Vibes → Modern trad pub

Less smart bistro, more proper Glasgow local with care taken. The existing venue trades as a daytime-to-late cafe-bar-restaurant. The offer reads scattered. Star wants it to compete properly in Shawlands' growing food and drink scene without losing the locals it already has.

A 1968 photo shows the same tenement signed as "The Oak Tree Inn" — real heritage we can mine.

Gut check

Could you read a book here all afternoon? Would the gilded window sign look at home next to Bennets, Bow Bar, Café Royal?

Reference pubs

Bennets · Bow Bar · Café Royal · Babbity Bowster · Sloans · Blackbird — Glasgow/Edinburgh trad pub vernacular: gilded glass, sandstone, dark wood, brass rail, mosaic tile threshold

Not

English home-counties gastropub pastiche. ESD is a starting reference, not a destination. Too Surrey to land in Shawlands as a Glasgow local.

Every painted timber surface, fascia panel, column, door surround, canopy band and lower window framing in deep matt Green Smoke. Hand-gilded 23ct gold-leaf signage to front shopfront windows — "The White Swan" wordmark centred on the larger panels, sized to read clearly from Pollokshaws Road.

Shopfront works
  • Full repairs to rotten timbers and fascia
  • Repaint frontage in Green Smoke
  • Remove every existing vinyl, menu box, amenity board
  • New gilded signage — 23ct gold leaf, inside of glass
  • Hanging painted-timber pub blade on wrought-iron bracket
  • Wrought-iron hanging baskets — ivy and trailing geraniums
  • Warm 2200K interior light spilling from front windows
  • Remove satellite dishes, Heineken sign, window clutter
The name

"Eala Bhán" is Scottish Gaelic for white swan — roughly "AY-luh VAHN." Nobody outside Gaelic speakers can pronounce it. The new name keeps the heritage, makes it legible to everyone, and looks right gilded onto a window.

Interior · Spatial moves

One continuous room

The big move: open the function area — strip the partition and door; make good. The room becomes one continuous run. A second servery in the extension with a feature metal back-bar set against exposed brick.

Material vocabulary

Distressed antique leather · Brass footrails · Antique brass ironmongery · Polished ash screens · Polished timber bar tops · Aged mirror and tiling to back-bar · Subtly distressed paint finishes · 2200K warm-white LED throughout · Feature wallpaper to upper toilet walls and dining-room frieze · Commercial-grade LVT in toilet wet areas

Dining area concept
