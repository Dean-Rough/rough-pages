Driftwood

Elevated Dive Bar — Glasgow

Driftwood reads as found, not styled. A late-night room that feels like it has always been there — moody, lived-in, and quietly confident. Layered botanicals, vintage pendants and warm-dim LED zoning carry the eye from the door to the back wall, where pinball and neon do the heavy lifting.

The pitch is tight: a maximalist, retro-gaming bar that earns its keep without renovating the bones. Investment is concentrated where the customer feels it — lighting, signage, surface texture — so every pound spent shows up at the table.

Design
Moves
Bang-for-buck. Investment where the customer feels it.
Move 01
Neon Anchor
A single hand-bent sign sets the room. Reads from the street, photographs at the booth.
Move 02
Warm-Dim Zoning
Three lighting layers — pendant, wall-wash, candle. Dimmed deliberately, not weakly.
Move 03
Botanical Layer
Dense planting against deep teal walls. Softens noise and reads lived-in by week one.
Move 04
Pinball Corner
Two cabinets, a vinyl shelf, one good speaker. Earns the back wall and pulls dwell time.
Move 05
Curated Wall
Salvaged folk art and signwritten panels. Tells a story without reading like a theme bar.
