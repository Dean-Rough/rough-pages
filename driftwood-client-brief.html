Driftwood

Elevated Dive Bar
Glasgow

Driftwood is a room that feels found, not furnished. A late-night bar where the mood does the work — moody, lived-in, quietly confident. Curated without trying. Atmospheric without being heavy. Built for the customer who wants a second drink before they have finished the first.

02 — Concept

The Elevated
Dive Bar

Not a dive bar. Not a cocktail bar. The space in between. Somewhere you can lean on the bar in a good coat or a worn-in jacket and feel equally at home. Pinball in the back. Neon over the bar. Greenery softening every hard edge.

The room earns its keep through atmosphere, not fit-out spend — every design decision concentrated where the customer feels it.

03 — Aesthetic

Maximalist
Botanical
and Retro Gaming

Maximalist Botanical × Retro Gaming

Two worlds, one room. Layered greenery — real where light allows, considered faux where it doesn’t — meets the warmth of analogue games. Pinball cabinets as anchor objects, sourced second-hand and restored. Neon as both light source and graphic statement. The density of a collected space, the restraint of a designed one.

04 — Palette

Deep · Desaturated
Forgiving

A palette that does two jobs at once: builds mood and hides budget. Deep teals and forest greens on walls absorb light and make the room feel smaller in the right way. Off-blacks on joinery. Chocolate as the warm bridge tone. A single neon accent — tangerine-led — cuts through the dark.

05 — Design Moves

Six Moves
That Make the Room

01

Neon Anchor

A single hand-bent neon piece behind the bar. Reads from the street, photographs at every table. One sign does the work of three downlights.

02

Warm-Dim Zoning

Three lighting circuits on independent dimming: entrance, bar, back room. The room should change shape across the evening without changing a single fitting.

03

Botanical Layering

Dense planting at sightline-breakers — bar back, booth ends, threshold. Softens noise, hides dated surfaces, reads lived-in by week one. Specified for low-light tolerance.

04

Vintage Pendant Cluster

Mismatched pendants over the bar pass. Coordinated by warmth and brass tones, sourced at auction over retail. No two the same, all reading as one family.

05

Pinball Corner

One restored pinball machine and a cocktail-table arcade cabinet. Generates dwell time and return visits without touching the drinks list. Second-hand, not showroom.

06

Joinery, Restrained

Existing bar retained where sound. Top re-clad, fascia restained, brass detailing added. New joinery confined to back-bar shelving and one banquette run. No structural moves unless they unlock revenue.

06 — Reference

The Lioness
of Leith

A bar that knows what it is and does not need to explain.

The single most accurate read on the elevated dive-bar mood. Object density without visual weight. Atmosphere through lighting, sound, and curation rather than fit-out. The benchmark we are aiming for in feel, not in detail.
